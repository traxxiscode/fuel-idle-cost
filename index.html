How This Dashboard Works

  • Date range: the dashboard reviews the last 3 months by default. Use Custom to select another date range for fuel, trip, idle, and stop activity.
  • Current view: every card, chart, hot spot, and vehicle result updates to match the group and efficiency filters you choose.
  • Total fuel used: adds up recorded fuel use across the current selected view.
  • Fleet MPG and vehicle MPG: uses trip distance divided by total fuel used, so idling affects MPG the same way it affects real-world fuel economy.
  • Idle fuel used: uses recorded idle fuel when available. If it is missing, the dashboard estimates idle fuel from idle time and the burn rate you set.
  • Idle cost: multiplies idle fuel by the fuel price you choose.
  • Idle hot spots: groups stopped locations where idling happened most often and lets you open each location to see which vehicles are driving the total.
  • Efficiency status: Top priority means idle cost is at least $250 or idle share is at least 30%. Monitor means idle cost is at least $100 or idle share is at least 15%. Strong efficiency means the vehicle is below those thresholds.
  • Trending: compares this month’s projected full-month pace with last month so you can quickly see whether fuel cost and idle cost are heading up or down.
  • CO2 produced: estimates tailpipe emissions from the fuel used and the fuel type you select.
